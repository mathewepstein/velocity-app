How the score is built

Each dev's composite score for the current window is a weighted sum of z-scored metrics — every contribution is measured against the rest of the active cohort, not against a fixed bar. Bigger weight = bigger impact on rank.

Bi-weekly Elo rating
Pairwise rating updates every two weeks: each dev's composite for the period defines a relative "result," and Elo moves their rating accordingly. K-factor tiers down as periods played grows, so short-tenured devs aren't punished by early swings. Provisional badge until they cross the configured periods threshold.
PRs merged ×3.0
The headline shipping signal. A merged PR is the strongest evidence the work landed.
Issues resolved ×2.0
Tickets actually closed by this dev in the window — caps "merged a PR but ticket still open" gaming.
code_impact ×1.5
Substance-of-contribution composite: sqrt(α·unique_files + β·loc_capped + γ·merged_prs). Combines breadth of files touched, lines changed (p95-capped), and shipping cadence into one depth-of-work number. Replaces raw commit counts in the score.
PR reviews ×1.0
Counts review submissions on others' PRs. Catches the reviewer-heavy devs who don't show up in raw output numbers.
PRs opened ×0.5
Created (not necessarily merged) — picks up devs whose work is mid-flight at the window cutoff.
Issues touched ×0.5
Any Jira activity on this dev's assigned tickets — surfacing engagement that hasn't crossed the "resolved" line yet.
Active weeks ×0.5
Distinct calendar weeks with at least one signal. Spreads credit across consistency, not just intensity.
Story points ×0.5
Sum of completed Jira story points — proxy for sized effort when teams use pointing.
LoC changed ×0.25
Added + deleted, p95-capped and sqrt-dampened so one giant refactor week doesn't dwarf everyone.
